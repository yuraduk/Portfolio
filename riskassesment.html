Overview

In a world where risks are constantly evolving, accurate and comprehensive risk assessments are crucial.
This project aimed to revolutionise the risk assessment process for one of the world’s largest reinsurance companies.
Leveraging cutting-edge technology and a user-centered design approach, a platform was developed to enable users to evaluate their assets' exposure to natural hazards and climate change, both presently and in the future.

Project Goals

The core objective was a complete overhaul of the existing risk assessment platform. Through thorough analysis of the current system, understanding user needs, and identifying key challenges, the focus was on enhancing usability and boosting user satisfaction.
New features and use cases were integrated based on user feedback, ensuring the platform aligns more closely with their requirements.
Ultimately, the goal was to create a dynamic, user-centric solution that addresses current pain points and adapts to evolving user needs.

Approach

The design process integrated Design Thinking with Lean UX strategies, emphasizing a deep understanding of users, business requirements, and the market landscape.

The process commenced with user research and ideation to generate diverse solutions through collaborative brainstorming. Transitioning to Lean UX, rapid prototypes were developed for iterative testing.
Close collaboration with the agile development team ensured prompt design refinements based on user feedback, facilitating adaptation to evolving project dynamics. In summary, the approach combined user-centricity, innovation, and rapid iterations to align the product with user preferences, business goals, and market trends.

Our target audience spanned diverse sectors, including insurance, financial institutions, real estate, and corporations. An in-depth discovery phase was conducted, involving evaluations of the current platform and stakeholder interviews. This approach captured varying expectations and concerns, emphasizing the need to address a broad spectrum of user needs—from simple interfaces for novices to powerful analytics tools for seasoned users.

User Groups & Journey Maps

Drawing from user and stakeholder interviews, distinct user groups with shared needs and interests were identified. These user segments form the basis for crafting personas and Customer Journey Maps, facilitating a deeper understanding of their unique experiences and requirements.
Customer Journey Maps visualize and illuminate the end-to-end user experience with a product or service. This visualization aids in identifying pain points, moments of delight, and areas for improvement throughout the user journey.
